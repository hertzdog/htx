The challenge

Healthcare needs AI. But not at any cost.

Artificial intelligence is transforming medicine: from diagnostic imaging to surgical planning, from genomic analysis to clinical decision support. The benefits are clear: reduced diagnostic variability, faster documentation, support in managing complex protocols.

But healthcare is also Europe's most regulated sector. Clinical data qualifies as special category data under GDPR (Article 9), subject to enhanced protection. The Medical Devices Regulation (MDR 2017/745) imposes strict requirements for software as a medical device. And the EU AI Act classifies healthcare AI as high-risk (Annex III, point 5), requiring transparency, human oversight, risk management, and data governance.

The result: healthcare organisations face a dilemma. On one hand, AI could significantly improve care quality and operational efficiency. On the other, most AI solutions available on the market — ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini — send data to US servers, making regulatory compliance impossible for European health data.

38.4% of published studies on LLM use in healthcare do not implement adequate patient data protections. This is not acceptable in a real clinical setting.

Case study

ChemoMaker and Biovalley: AI for clinical protocols.

The partnership between HTX, Biovalley Investments, and the ChemoMaker project represents a concrete example of AI applied to clinical protocols in oncology.

ChemoMaker is a robot for automated preparation of chemotherapy drugs — a process requiring maximum precision and traceability. HTX integrated MANTA to enable natural language querying of pharmaceutical production databases: preparation times, dosages, batches, anomalies.

Healthcare staff can ask "Which preparations exceeded standard times last week?" and receive precise answers in seconds, without SQL skills or IT department involvement.

This is a concrete case of how private AI — deployed on-premise, with data never leaving the facility's perimeter — can improve clinical safety and operational efficiency in a high-criticality setting.

Compliance

GDPR, MDR, AI Act: compliance by design.

In healthcare, regulatory compliance is not a nice-to-have — it is a non-negotiable prerequisite. Here is how PRISMA, the infrastructure powering KOI and ORCA, ensures compliance with all relevant regulations.

GDPR

Personal data protection

Clinical data is special category data (Art. 9 GDPR) requiring the highest level of protection. With PRISMA, all data stays on-premise within your hospital infrastructure. No data is transferred to external servers. No data is used to train third-party models. End-to-end encryption across the entire pipeline.

Art. 9 GDPR
MDR

Medical devices

The Medical Devices Regulation (MDR 2017/745) imposes specific requirements for Software as a Medical Device (SaMD). KOI is currently classified as Research Use Only (RUO) and is pursuing ISO 13485 and IEC 62304 certification for medical device marking planned for 2027. Every development phase follows quality by design principles.

ISO 13485 in progress
AI ACT

EU AI regulation

The AI Act classifies healthcare AI as high-risk (Annex III). This requires: algorithmic transparency, human oversight, documented risk management, data governance, and post-market monitoring. KOI is designed with native human-in-the-loop: the physician sees the AI's reasoning and always decides independently.

High-risk — Annex III

Research

Grounded in scientific research.

KOI is not a product born from a marketing idea. It is the result of a research project funded by the Friuli Venezia Giulia Region (LR 22/2022, art. 7), developed in collaboration with Ospedale del Quadrante (Ramsay Sante) for clinical validation.

The HTX study evaluated 11 language models on 20 standardised clinical vignettes from the most cited ASA-PS benchmarks in the literature. Results demonstrate that advanced reasoning models significantly outperform both first-generation models and average human performance.

This scientific approach — rigorous validation, standardised benchmarks, peer-reviewed publication — is what distinguishes a serious clinical AI product from a technology demo.

FAQ — AI for Healthcare

Are HTX healthcare AI solutions GDPR and AI Act compliant?

Yes. All HTX solutions run on PRISMA, our private infrastructure with end-to-end encryption. Clinical data never leaves the hospital premises. No data is sent to external servers or used to train third-party models. GDPR, MDR, and AI Act compliant by design.

No, and it is not designed to. KOI is a clinical decision support system that reduces inter-observer variability in ASA-PS classification. The final decision always rests with the physician. KOI provides an AI-based second opinion to support, not replace, clinical judgement.
